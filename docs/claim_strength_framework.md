# Claim Strength Framework

The evidence matrix uses two rating fields to keep report language aligned with the underlying support.

## Evidence Strength

- `Strong`: Direct evidence from a credible source, with clear metric support.
- `Medium`: Useful evidence, but with some limits in scope, method, age, or transferability.
- `Contextual`: Background or operational evidence that supports context but not a quantified economic claim by itself.
- `Modeled`: Scenario, model, estimate, or projection rather than directly observed impact.
- `Needs verification`: A claim or metric that should be checked against the original source before use.

## IOOS Attribution Strength

- `Strong`: The source directly connects the impact or decision support to an IOOS component.
- `Medium`: The IOOS link is plausible and documented, but not the only driver of the outcome.
- `Contextual`: The source supports the setting or service role, but attribution is indirect.
- `Modeled`: Attribution depends on model assumptions or scenario logic.
- `Needs verification`: The IOOS connection needs confirmation before report use.

## Claim Language

Use cautious language when evidence or attribution is below `Strong`. Prefer terms such as "supports," "can help," "is associated with," "estimated," "modeled," or "potential" unless the source directly supports a stronger causal claim.
